# Basin of Attraction and Operational Accessibility

The basin of attraction represents the operationally accessible region of initial conditions from which system dynamics converge toward a retained operational regime.

Within the framework, basin accessibility determines whether retained synthesis continuity is dynamically reachable under endogenous operational evolution.

# Operational Basin Structure

Let:

`A`

represent a retained operational attractor.

The basin of attraction:

`B(A)`

represents the set of operational states satisfying:

`x(t) → A`

over operational time.

Operationally this means:

- operational trajectories converge toward retained continuity;

- recursive structural organization remains dynamically accessible;

- synthesis-cycle retention becomes operationally viable.

# Operational Accessibility

A retained operational regime must possess a non-zero basin of attraction.

Operationally this means:

- retained synthesis continuity must remain physically reachable;

- operational organization must emerge through admissible system evolution;

- retention cannot represent an unreachable isolated configuration.

Within the framework:

- operational accessibility determines whether retained continuity may emerge under realistic dynamic conditions.

# Relation to Structural Balance

Basin accessibility remains operationally constrained by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

where:

- C(t) — general endogenous structural coherence parameter;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure, fragmentation pressure, structural load, or dissipative maintenance cost.

If positive structural balance and endogenous structural coherence remain preserved:

- basin accessibility remains stable;

- operational convergence toward retained continuity remains viable;

- recursive synthesis accessibility increases.

If structural imbalance dominates:

- basin accessibility compresses;

- convergence probability decreases;

- instability propagation increases.

This condition must not be reduced to formal structural existence alone.

A system may preserve positive structural balance temporarily while basin accessibility is already shrinking due to degradation of endogenous structural coherence.

Real dynamic stability remains governed by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

# Basin Compression Near Criticality

Near critical operational regimes:

- basin accessibility decreases;

- stabilization accessibility compresses;

- operational sensitivity increases;

- bifurcation accessibility expands.

Operationally this means:

- smaller perturbations may destabilize retained continuity;

- transition trajectories become increasingly sensitive to endogenous drift.

Near the critical boundary:

`C(t) ≈ P(t)`

small parameter changes may produce disproportionate structural consequences.

This is the EDC-sensitive region where basin accessibility becomes highly dependent on the qualitative characteristics of endogenous drift.

# Recursive Basin Evolution

Within recursively inherited synthesis cycles:

- basin topology evolves dynamically;

- inherited attractor deformation modifies future accessibility;

- recursive instability accumulation changes future convergence probability.

Therefore:

- operational accessibility becomes history-dependent;

- prior synthesis continuity influences future basin structure.

Recursive basin evolution does not mean mechanical repetition.

It means that prior retained synthesis cycles, attractor topology, accumulated positive structural work, and instability gradients modify future accessibility of retained operational regimes.

The system inherits the qualitative characteristics of prior retained regimes.

These qualitative characteristics may include:

- basin size;

- basin stability;

- sensitivity to perturbation;

- direction of endogenous drift;

- accessibility of subsequent resonance windows;

- and probability of convergence toward the next retained operational domain.

# Resonance Accessibility

Basin accessibility remains dynamically connected to resonance accessibility.

Within stable operational basins:

- resonance-window accessibility remains viable;

- synthesis-cycle continuation remains dynamically reachable;

- recursive stabilization remains accessible.

Near destabilized basins:

- resonance accessibility compresses;

- retained continuity weakens;

- operational instability increases.

A resonance window may open transition possibilities, but retained synthesis requires that trajectories remain dynamically reachable inside a non-zero basin of attraction.

# Attractor Accessibility

Operational basins determine accessibility of:

- retained synthesis cycles;

- resonance-window trajectories;

- recursive continuity regimes;

- operational stabilization pathways.

Different attractors may possess:

- different accessibility volume;

- different stabilization accessibility;

- different bifurcation sensitivity;

- different qualitative characteristics of retained regimes.

These qualitative characteristics determine whether a retained operational regime supports future synthesis, reorganization, degradation, or collapse.

# Operational Interpretation

Within the framework, basin of attraction represents:

- operational accessibility of retained synthesis continuity;

- dynamic reachability of structural self-organization;

- recursive convergence toward retained operational regimes;

- accessibility boundaries of nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems;

- and inherited qualitative characteristics of future operational accessibility.

A retained synthesis cycle is dynamically meaningful only if its attractor has a non-zero basin of attraction and remains accessible under the condition:

`C(t) > P(t)`
