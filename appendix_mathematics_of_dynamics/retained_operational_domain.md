# Retained Operational Domain

The retained operational domain represents the dynamically stable operational region in which structural continuity remains preserved after reduction or removal of external forcing.

Within the framework, retained operational domains define the conditions under which synthesis cycles preserve continuity through endogenous operational dynamics.

---

# Operational Domain Structure

Let:

    Ω(t)

represent the dynamically accessible operational state-space domain of the system.

A retained operational domain is defined as:

    Ω_ret ⊆ Ω(t)

where:

- Ω_ret — retained operational continuity region;
- Ω(t) — total dynamically accessible operational domain.

Operationally:

- Ω_ret represents the region in which retained synthesis continuity remains dynamically viable.

---

# Retention Condition

A synthesis cycle is considered operationally retained only if:

    ∃ τ > 0

such that:

    ∀ t ≥ t₀ + τ : x(t) ∈ Ω_ret

after reduction or removal of external forcing.

Operationally this means:

- structural continuity persists without continuous external stabilization;
- recursive operational continuity remains preserved;
- endogenous dynamics sustain retained organization.

---

# Relation to Structural Balance

Retention accessibility remains operationally constrained by:

    C(t) > P(t)

If positive structural balance remains preserved:

- retained synthesis continuity remains viable;
- operational trajectories remain bounded;
- recursive continuity stabilizes.

If structural imbalance dominates:

- retained continuity destabilizes;
- operational trajectories escape retained accessibility;
- recursive fragmentation increases.

---

# Dynamic Accessibility

The retained operational domain must remain dynamically accessible.

Operationally this means:

- the retained regime must be physically reachable;
- operational continuity must emerge through admissible system evolution;
- retention cannot represent an unreachable isolated singularity.
