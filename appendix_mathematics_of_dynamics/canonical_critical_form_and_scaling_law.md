# Canonical Critical Form and Scaling Law

This appendix describes the canonical critical reduction and the scaling behavior of endogenous dynamic criticality in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, scaling behavior emerges from endogenous drift near critical operational regimes.

---

# Critical Dynamic Regime

Near criticality, structural balance dynamics are approximated by:

    dC/dt = vtC − C³

where:

- C(t) — accumulated structural balance;
- v — endogenous drift velocity toward the bifurcation region.

The cubic term represents nonlinear saturation of structural growth.

---

# Endogenous Drift Approximation

Near the critical regime:

    r(t) ≈ vt

where:

- r(t) — endogenous control parameter;
- v — endogenous drift velocity.

Within the framework:

    v = μP

where:

- μ — coupling coefficient;
- P — structural load and maintenance cost.

Substitution produces:

    dC/dt = vtC − C³

---

# Canonical Rescaling

Introduce rescaled variables:

    C = v^(1/3)y

    t = v^(−1/3)τ

The system reduces to the canonical critical form:

    dy/dτ = τy − y³

Operationally this represents the parameter-reduced universal structure of the critical regime.

---

# Scaling Law

The characteristic transition delay follows:

    t_delay ~ v^(−1/3)

Operationally this means:

- increasing endogenous drift velocity reduces stabilization accessibility time;
- decreasing drift velocity increases adaptive stabilization accessibility.

---

# Structural Interpretation

Within the framework:

- slow endogenous drift supports retained synthesis accessibility;
- accelerated drift compresses resonance-window accessibility;
- excessive drift increases bifurcation sensitivity;
- delayed stabilization increases operational instability.

Operationally this means:

- stabilization cost grows nonlinearly near criticality.

---

# Recursive Scaling Effects

Scaling behavior influences not only the current transition regime.

It also modifies:

- future resonance-window accessibility;
- recursive synthesis continuity;
- inherited drift conditions;
- stabilization accessibility of subsequent synthesis cycles.

Therefore:

- current drift conditions influence future operational accessibility.

---

# Relation to Structural Balance

Scaling accessibility remains operationally constrained by:

    C(t) > P(t)

If positive structural balance remains preserved:

- retained synthesis continuity remains viable;
- stabilization accessibility remains operationally available.

If structural imbalance dominates:

- drift accelerates;
- stabilization intervals compress;
- destructive bifurcation accessibility increases.

---

# Universality Conditions

This scaling form applies when:

- nonlinear cubic saturation dominates;
- endogenous drift remains locally smooth;
- the control parameter evolves near criticality;
- the system remains nonlinear, open, dissipative, and dynamically evolving.

Outside these conditions, alternative scaling behavior may emerge.

---

Within the framework, canonical scaling law represents:

- nonlinear compression of stabilization accessibility;
