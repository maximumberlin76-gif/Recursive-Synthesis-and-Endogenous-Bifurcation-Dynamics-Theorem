# Recursive Synthesis and Endogenous Bifurcation Dynamics Theorem

This repository presents a theorem-layer framework dedicated to the investigation of recursive dependence between completed synthesis cycles, endogenous bifurcation dynamics, and the inherited quality of subsequent resonance windows.

The framework does not interpret synthesis as an isolated finalized event.

Instead, synthesis is treated as a dynamic process whose completed cycles recursively modify the conditions governing subsequent stages of self-organization.

Within this approach, each completed synthesis cycle leaves behind inherited structural quality influencing:

- the configuration of the next resonance window of phase transition toward a new level of structural self-organization,
- the direction and velocity of bifurcation drift,
- retention stability,
- attractor topology,
- system sensitivity to subsequent transitions,
- and the potential of future synthesis cycles.

The repository develops the operational logic of:

- completed synthesis cycles,
- recursive inheritance of the quality of completed synthesis cycles,
- drift accumulation,
- conditions governing the quality, properties, and velocity of bifurcation drift,
- the conditions and quality of emerging resonance windows,
- attractor transformation dynamics,
- and endogenous evolution of bifurcation dynamics.

Core framework thesis:

a completed synthesis cycle does not terminate the dynamic process of self-organization.

It defines the configuration of qualities, properties, and velocity of endogenous drift while simultaneously shaping the configurational characteristics of the next synthesis cycle.

# External Initiation and Self-Sustained Endogenous Dynamics

## Operational Definition

Within this framework, external initiation is considered not as the creation of endogenous evolutionary synthesis, but as the initiation of conditions under which the system may enter the first self-sustained endogenous cycle of structural self-organization.

An external impulse is capable of initiating:

- entry of the system into an admissible dynamic regime,
- accumulation of endogenous structural integrity,
- formation of a primary endogenous attractor,
- and initiation of the first cycle of endogenous structural synthesis.

However, subsequent stability of the system is determined no longer by the external impulse, but by the quality of the formed endogenous dynamics of the system.

---

# Insufficiency of a Short External Impulse

A short external impulse may induce temporary phase synchronization, but this is insufficient for formation of a stable regime of endogenous structural synthesis.

If the duration of external initiation is insufficient,

the system does not have enough time to:

- accumulate endogenous structural integrity,
- complete the first completed cycle of endogenous synthesis,
- form a stable endogenous attractor,
- and transition into a self-sustained regime of endogenous dynamic stability.

In such a case, after removal of the external impulse, the system returns to the previous regime of dissipative degradation.

---

# The First Completed Endogenous Cycle

The first completed endogenous cycle emerges only when external initiation is maintained long enough for the system to pass through a complete operational phase interval of accumulation, retention, and formation of an endogenous attractor.

The critical condition is:

- C(t) > R(t) over time

across a completed operational phase interval.

If this condition is satisfied,

the system obtains the capability to transition from an externally initiated regime toward self-sustained endogenous dynamics.

---

# Formation of an Endogenous Attractor

An endogenous attractor is formed as a result of:

- accumulation of endogenous structural integrity,
- retention of positive structural balance,
- stabilization of the regime of endogenous dynamic stability,
- and completion of the first cycle of endogenous structural synthesis.

After formation of the endogenous attractor, the system no longer directly depends on the external impulse as a permanent source of retention.

External initiation performs the role of activation.

The endogenous attractor performs the role of an internal retention mechanism.

---

# Removal of External Influence

After removal of external influence, the system can preserve stability only if a self-sustained endogenous attractor has already formed within the system.

If the endogenous attractor is formed,

the system continues to retain:

- endogenous structural integrity,
- positive structural balance,
- the regime of endogenous dynamic stability,
- and recursive continuity of endogenous structural synthesis.

If the endogenous attractor is not formed,

the system loses stability almost immediately after removal of external influence.

---

# Reverse Recursive Decay

If, after removal of external influence, the endogenous attractor gradually loses retention,

decay occurs not instantaneously, but through reverse recursive deformation.

Within such a regime, the system undergoes:

- reduction of density of endogenous structural coherence,
- weakening of retention of structural integrity,
- deformation of endogenous bifurcation drift,
- reduction of accessibility of resonance windows of phase-transition cycles,
- and gradual loss of the regime of endogenous dynamic stability.

Thus, collapse of the regime is not an instantaneous event.

It manifests as recursive decay of previously formed endogenous dynamics.

Recursive decay of the endogenous attractor creates an observable temporal safety window.

- reduction of endogenous coherence,
- deformation of attractor topology,
