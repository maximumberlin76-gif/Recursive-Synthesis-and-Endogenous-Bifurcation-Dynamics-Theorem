# Resonance-Window Compression Near Criticality

Resonance-window compression describes the progressive reduction of operational resonance accessibility as a nonlinear open dissipative dynamic system approaches critical destabilization regimes.

Within the framework, resonance accessibility is not static.

Near criticality, endogenous drift, perturbation sensitivity, and recursive instability accumulation progressively reduce the operational accessibility of resonance windows.

# Fundamental Compression Condition

Resonance accessibility compresses as the system approaches:

`C(t) → P(t)`

where:

- C(t) — general endogenous structural coherence parameter;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure, fragmentation pressure, structural load, or dissipative maintenance cost.

Operationally this means:

- endogenous structural coherence weakens relative to destabilizing structural pressure;

- stabilization accessibility decreases;

- endogenous instability increases;

- retained synthesis continuity becomes increasingly fragile.

This condition must not be reduced to phase synchronization alone.

Phase synchronization may weaken near criticality, but the deeper compression condition is the approach of endogenous structural coherence toward destabilizing pressure:

`C(t) → P(t)`

# Compression Dynamics

Near criticality:

- resonance-window accessibility intervals narrow;

- coherent accumulation destabilizes;

- phase-synchronization / coherence-support continuity weakens;

- perturbation sensitivity increases.

Operationally this corresponds to:

- progressive reduction of operational resonance accessibility.

# Relation to Endogenous Drift

Compression intensity depends on endogenous drift velocity.

Near accelerated drift regimes:

- stabilization intervals compress;

- resonance accessibility narrows faster;

- bifurcation accessibility expands;

- recursive instability propagates more rapidly.

Operationally this means:

- excessive endogenous drift reduces constructive synthesis accessibility.

# Scaling Relation

Resonance compression remains dynamically linked to:

`t_delay ~ v^(−1/3)`

Operationally this means:

- increasing endogenous drift velocity decreases operational resonance accessibility time;

- delayed stabilization compresses constructive operational accessibility.

The scaling relation does not define resonance-window compression by itself.

It describes how endogenous drift velocity affects the available delay before critical transition development.

# Coherent Accumulation Compression

Near critical operational regimes:

- coherent accumulation becomes increasingly unstable;

- phase-synchronization-assisted structural continuity weakens;

- retained synthesis accessibility destabilizes.

Operationally this means:

- constructive operational accumulation becomes more difficult to preserve.

However:

- phase synchronization is not identical to endogenous structural coherence;

- R(t), if used, remains a synchronization / coherence-support indicator;

- retained synthesis requires accumulated positive structural work and preservation of `C(t) > P(t)` over operational time.

# Recursive Compression Dynamics

Within recursively inherited synthesis cycles:

- resonance compression propagates recursively;

- inherited instability gradients modify future resonance accessibility;

- recursive attractor deformation reduces future stabilization accessibility.

Therefore:

- resonance accessibility compression becomes history-dependent.

Recursive compression does not mean mechanical repetition.

It means that prior retained synthesis cycles, instability gradients, and attractor deformation modify future resonance-window accessibility.

# Oscillatory Phase-Synchronization Destabilization

Near criticality:

- oscillatory phase-synchronization regimes destabilize;

- phase coordination weakens;

- cyclic continuity becomes increasingly fragile.

Operationally this corresponds to:

- degradation of phase-synchronization-assisted stabilization accessibility.

This destabilization may indicate loss of coherence-support dynamics, but it is not identical to the full loss of endogenous structural coherence.

Real dynamic stability remains governed by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

# Resonance Accessibility Boundaries

Near compressed resonance regimes:

- operational accessibility becomes increasingly sensitive to perturbation;

- retained synthesis continuity becomes less stable;

- transition trajectories become more difficult to control.

Operationally this means:

- operational flexibility decreases near critical accessibility boundaries.

A compressed resonance window is not automatically destructive.

Its qualitative sign depends on the direction and quality of endogenous drift.

If endogenous structural coherence can be restored above destabilizing pressure, the system may still enter a retained synthesis or reorganization pathway.

If not, compression may lead toward degradation or collapse.

# Operational Interpretation

Within the framework, resonance-window compression near criticality represents:

- progressive reduction of resonance accessibility;

- destabilization of coherent accumulation continuity;

- weakening of phase-synchronization / coherence-support dynamics;

- recursive weakening of synthesis accessibility;

- and compression of operational phase-transition accessibility in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

The core condition remains:

`C(t) → P(t)`

as the system approaches the critical boundary,

and:

`C(t) > P(t)`

as the condition for retained dynamic stability.
