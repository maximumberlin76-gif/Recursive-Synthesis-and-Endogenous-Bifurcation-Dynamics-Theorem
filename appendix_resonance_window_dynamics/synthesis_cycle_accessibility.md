# Synthesis-Cycle Accessibility

Synthesis-cycle accessibility describes the operational conditions under which retained synthesis cycles become dynamically reachable within nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, synthesis accessibility is not treated as an instantaneous event or isolated synchronization effect.

A synthesis cycle represents a dynamically retained operational process emerging through coherent accumulation, resonance accessibility, structural balance preservation, and recursive continuity stabilization.

---

# Fundamental Accessibility Condition

Synthesis-cycle accessibility remains operationally possible only while:

    C(t) > P(t)

over sufficiently preserved operational intervals.

Operationally this means:

- structural integrity accumulation exceeds structural losses;
- retained operational continuity remains viable;
- endogenous instability remains bounded;
- recursive synthesis accessibility remains dynamically accessible.

---

# Coherent Accumulation Requirement

Synthesis accessibility additionally requires:

    Θ_N ≥ Θ_crit

where:

- Θ_N — accumulated positive structural work;
- Θ_crit — minimum coherent accumulation threshold required for retained synthesis accessibility.

Within the framework:

- temporary synchronization alone is insufficient;
- retained synthesis accessibility requires constructive accumulation continuity over operational time.

Synthesis cycles become dynamically accessible only within operational resonance regions.

- resonance windows support operational accessibility of synthesis-cycle formation.
