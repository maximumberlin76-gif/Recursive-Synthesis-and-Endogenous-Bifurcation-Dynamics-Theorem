# Synthesis-Cycle Accessibility

Synthesis-cycle accessibility describes the operational conditions under which retained synthesis cycles become dynamically reachable within nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, synthesis accessibility is not treated as an instantaneous event or isolated synchronization effect.

A synthesis cycle represents a dynamically retained operational process emerging through coherent accumulation, resonance accessibility, positive structural balance preservation, endogenous structural coherence, and recursive continuity stabilization.

# Fundamental Accessibility Condition

Synthesis-cycle accessibility remains operationally possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

over sufficiently preserved operational intervals.

where:

- C(t) — general endogenous structural coherence parameter;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure, fragmentation pressure, structural load, or dissipative maintenance cost.

Operationally this means:

- endogenous structural coherence and regenerative support exceed destabilizing structural pressure;

- retained operational continuity remains viable;

- endogenous instability remains bounded;

- recursive synthesis accessibility remains dynamically accessible.

This condition must not be reduced to phase synchronization alone.

Phase synchronization may support synthesis-cycle accessibility, but real dynamic stability remains governed by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

# Coherent Accumulation Requirement

Synthesis accessibility additionally requires:

`Θ_N ≥ Θ_crit`

where:

- Θ_N — accumulated positive structural work;

- Θ_crit — minimum coherent accumulation threshold required for retained synthesis accessibility.

Within the framework:

- temporary synchronization alone is insufficient;

- retained synthesis accessibility requires constructive accumulation continuity over operational time;

- accumulated positive structural work must be evaluated across completed operational intervals, not isolated instantaneous responses.

# Resonance Accessibility

Synthesis cycles become dynamically accessible only within operational resonance regions.

Near resonance accessibility:

- coherent accumulation stabilizes;

- oscillatory phase synchronization / coherence-support dynamics may intensify;

- recursive continuity accessibility increases;

- structural self-organization becomes dynamically reinforced.

Operationally this means:

- resonance windows support operational accessibility of synthesis-cycle formation.

However:

- increased phase synchronization does not by itself prove synthesis;

- R(t), if used, remains a synchronization / coherence-support indicator;

- retained synthesis requires accumulated positive structural work and preservation of `C(t) > P(t)` over operational time.

# Dynamic Accessibility Factors

Synthesis-cycle accessibility depends on:

- endogenous drift conditions;

- perturbation sensitivity;

- resonance accessibility stability;

- recursive continuity preservation;

- attractor topology;

- and retained operational accessibility.

Operationally this means:

- synthesis accessibility evolves dynamically rather than remaining fixed.

# Recursive Accessibility Dynamics

Within recursively inherited synthesis cycles:

- prior synthesis continuity influences future synthesis accessibility;

- inherited attractor topology modifies future resonance accessibility;

- recursive instability accumulation changes future stabilization accessibility.

Therefore:

- synthesis accessibility becomes recursively history-dependent.

Recursive inheritance does not mean mechanical repetition.

It means that prior retained synthesis cycles, attractor topology, accumulated positive structural work, and instability gradients modify future synthesis-cycle accessibility.

# Accessibility Compression Near Criticality

Near critical operational regimes:

- synthesis accessibility compresses;

- resonance-window accessibility narrows;

- coherent accumulation destabilizes;

- perturbation sensitivity increases.

Operationally this means:

- delayed stabilization decreases probability of constructive synthesis accessibility.

Near the critical boundary:

`C(t) ≈ P(t)`

small parameter changes may produce disproportionate structural consequences.

This is the EDC-sensitive region where synthesis-cycle accessibility becomes highly dependent on endogenous drift quality.

# Oscillatory Accessibility

Oscillatory phase-synchronization regimes may increase synthesis accessibility through:

- cyclic coherence-support stabilization;

- phase-synchronization-assisted constructive accumulation;

- recursive continuity reinforcement.

Operationally this corresponds to:

- increased accessibility of retained synthesis continuity.

However:

- oscillatory phase synchronization is not identical to endogenous structural coherence;

- R(t), if used, remains a synchronization / coherence-support indicator;

- retained synthesis requires accumulated positive structural work and preservation of `C(t) > P(t)` over operational time.

# Retained Synthesis Accessibility

A synthesis cycle becomes operationally retained only if:

- constructive accumulation remains sufficiently preserved;

- recursive continuity stabilizes;

- resonance accessibility persists after external forcing reduction;

- operational trajectories remain dynamically bounded;

- the system remains inside a retained operational domain;

- endogenous structural coherence remains stronger than destabilizing pressure.

Operationally this corresponds to:

- formation of retained structural self-organization continuity.

A retained synthesis cycle may be represented operationally as persistence inside:

`Ω_ret ⊆ Ω(t)`

after reduction or removal of external forcing.

This means:

- resonance-window entry gives access;

- accumulated positive structural work gives admission;

- retention inside Ω_ret proves operational persistence.

# Operational Interpretation

Within the framework, synthesis-cycle accessibility represents:

- operational accessibility of retained structural self-organization;

- resonance-assisted synthesis continuity;

- recursive formation of dynamically retained operational regimes;

- phase-synchronization-supported transition accessibility;

- and accessibility of constructive phase-transition dynamics in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Synthesis-cycle accessibility is not the same as temporary synchronization.

It requires:

- accumulated positive structural work;

- retained-domain accessibility;

- recursive continuity stabilization;

- and preservation of real dynamic stability:

`C(t) > P(t)`
