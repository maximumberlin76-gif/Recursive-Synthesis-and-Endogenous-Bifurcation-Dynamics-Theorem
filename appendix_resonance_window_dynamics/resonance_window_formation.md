# Resonance Window Formation

Resonance-window formation describes the emergence of operationally accessible dynamic regions in which retained synthesis cycles and phase-transition accessibility become possible within nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, resonance windows are not treated as simple frequency coincidence or temporary synchronization effects.

A resonance window represents a dynamically accessible operational region formed through coherent accumulation, positive structural balance preservation, endogenous structural coherence, endogenous drift conditions, and recursive continuity accessibility.

# Operational Formation Condition

Resonance-window accessibility becomes operationally possible only while:

`C(t) > P(t)`

over sufficiently preserved operational intervals.

where:

- C(t) — general endogenous structural coherence parameter;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure, fragmentation pressure, structural load, or dissipative maintenance cost.

Operationally this means:

- endogenous structural coherence and regenerative support exceed destabilizing structural pressure;

- retained operational continuity remains accessible;

- endogenous instability remains bounded;

- recursive synthesis accessibility remains viable.

This condition must not be reduced to phase synchronization alone.

Phase synchronization may support resonance-window formation, but real dynamic stability remains governed by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

# Coherent Accumulation

Resonance-window formation requires accumulated coherent structural contribution.

Operationally this corresponds to:

`Θ_N ≥ Θ_crit`

where:

- Θ_N — accumulated positive structural work;

- Θ_crit — minimum operational accumulation threshold required for resonance accessibility.

Within the framework:

- temporary synchronization alone is insufficient;

- resonance accessibility requires retained coherent accumulation over operational time;

- accumulated positive structural work must be evaluated across completed operational intervals, not isolated instantaneous responses.

# Resonance Accessibility

A resonance window represents an operational region in which:

- phase-synchronization accessibility may increase;

- synthesis-cycle formation becomes dynamically possible;

- structural self-organization intensifies;

- recursive continuity may reorganize.

Operationally this means:

- the system becomes dynamically sensitive to coherent operational interaction.

However:

- increased phase synchronization does not by itself prove synthesis;

- R(t), if used, remains a synchronization / coherence-support indicator;

- retained synthesis requires accumulated positive structural work and preservation of `C(t) > P(t)` over operational time.

# Relation to Endogenous Drift

Resonance-window formation depends on endogenous drift conditions.

Near excessive endogenous drift:

- resonance accessibility compresses;

- stabilization accessibility decreases;

- perturbation sensitivity increases.

Near stable drift conditions:

- resonance accessibility broadens;

- coherent accumulation stabilizes;

- synthesis accessibility increases.

The qualitative sign of the resonance window depends on the direction and quality of endogenous drift.

A resonance window may therefore become:

- a synthesis window;

- a reorganization window;

- a degradation window;

- or a collapse window.

# Oscillatory Accessibility

Near resonance accessibility regions:

- oscillatory phase synchronization may intensify;

- cyclic operational continuity may emerge;

- recursive synthesis continuity may stabilize.

Operationally this corresponds to:

- formation of dynamically retained oscillatory operational structures.

Oscillatory phase synchronization is a support mechanism for coherent accumulation and retained synthesis accessibility.

It is not identical to endogenous structural coherence C(t).

# Recursive Resonance Formation

Within recursively inherited synthesis cycles:

- resonance accessibility evolves dynamically;

- inherited attractor topology modifies future resonance accessibility;

- recursive continuity influences future synthesis accessibility.

Therefore:

- resonance-window formation becomes recursively history-dependent.

Recursive formation does not mean mechanical repetition.

It means that prior retained synthesis cycles, attractor topology, accumulated structural work, and instability gradients modify future resonance-window accessibility.

# Resonance Compression Near Criticality

Near critical operational regimes:

- resonance-window accessibility intervals narrow;

- coherent accumulation becomes less stable;

- perturbation sensitivity increases;

- retained synthesis accessibility weakens.

Operationally this means:

- stabilization accessibility decreases near bifurcation accessibility boundaries.

Near the critical boundary:

`C(t) ≈ P(t)`

small parameter changes may produce disproportionate structural consequences.

This is the EDC-sensitive region where resonance-window formation becomes highly dependent on endogenous drift quality.

# Operational Interpretation

Within the framework, resonance-window formation represents:

- emergence of operational accessibility regions for structural self-organization;

- dynamically accessible synthesis continuity;

- coherent accumulation accessibility;

- phase-synchronization-supported resonance accessibility;

- and operational phase-transition accessibility in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

The core condition remains:

`C(t) > P(t)`

Resonance-window formation is therefore not arbitrary access to transition.

It is dynamically constrained accessibility to structural reorganization under sufficient endogenous structural coherence, accumulated positive structural work, and retained operational continuity.
