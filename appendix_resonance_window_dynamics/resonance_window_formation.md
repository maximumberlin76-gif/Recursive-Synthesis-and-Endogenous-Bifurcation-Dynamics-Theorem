# Resonance Window Formation

Resonance-window formation describes the emergence of operationally accessible dynamic regions in which retained synthesis cycles and phase-transition accessibility become possible within nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, resonance windows are not treated as simple frequency coincidence or temporary synchronization effects.

A resonance window represents a dynamically accessible operational region formed through coherent accumulation, structural balance preservation, endogenous drift conditions, and recursive continuity accessibility.

---

# Operational Formation Condition

Resonance-window accessibility becomes operationally possible only while:

    C(t) > P(t)

over sufficiently preserved operational intervals.

Operationally this means:

- structural integrity accumulation exceeds structural losses;
- retained operational continuity remains accessible;
- endogenous instability remains bounded;
- recursive synthesis accessibility remains viable.

---

# Coherent Accumulation

Resonance-window formation requires accumulated coherent structural contribution.

Operationally this corresponds to:

    Θ_N ≥ Θ_crit

where:

- Θ_N — accumulated positive structural work;
- Θ_crit — minimum operational accumulation threshold required for resonance accessibility.

Within the framework:

- temporary synchronization alone is insufficient;
- resonance accessibility requires retained coherent accumulation over operational time.

---

# Resonance Accessibility

A resonance window represents an operational region in which:

- synchronization accessibility increases;
- synthesis-cycle formation becomes dynamically possible;
- structural self-organization intensifies;
- recursive continuity may reorganize.

Operationally this means:

- the system becomes dynamically sensitive to coherent operational interaction.

# Relation to Endogenous Drift

Resonance-window formation depends on endogenous drift conditions.

Near excessive endogenous drift:

- resonance accessibility compresses;
- stabilization accessibility decreases;
- perturbation sensitivity increases.

Near stable drift conditions:

- resonance accessibility broadens;
- coherent accumulation stabilizes;
- synthesis accessibility increases.

---

Near resonance accessibility regions:

- oscillatory synchronization may intensify;

Operationally this corresponds to:

- formation of dynamically retained oscillatory operational structures.

# Recursive Resonance Formation

Within recursively inherited synthesis cycles:

- resonance accessibility evolves dynamically;

- recursive continuity influences future synthesis accessibility.

Therefore:

- resonance-window formation becomes recursively history-dependent.

---

# Resonance Compression Near Criticality

Near critical operational regimes:

- resonance-window accessibility intervals narrow;
- coherent accumulation becomes less stable;
- perturbation sensitivity increases;
- retained synthesis accessibility weakens.

Operationally this means:

- stabilization accessibility decreases near bifurcation accessibility boundaries.

Within the framework, resonance-window formation represents:

- emergence of operational accessibility regions for structural self-organization;
