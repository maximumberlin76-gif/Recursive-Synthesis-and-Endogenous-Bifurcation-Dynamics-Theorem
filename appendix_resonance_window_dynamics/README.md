## Purpose

This appendix formalizes the operational mechanisms governing formation, accessibility, retention, and deformation of resonance windows in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

Within the framework, a resonance window is not treated as simple frequency coincidence or temporary synchronization.

A resonance window represents an operationally accessible dynamic region in which retained synthesis cycles and phase-transition accessibility become dynamically possible under sufficient positive structural balance, accumulated positive structural work, and endogenous structural coherence conditions.

The appendix expands the resonance layer of the framework by describing:

- endogenous resonance accessibility;

- resonance-assisted synthesis dynamics;

- coherent operational accumulation;

- oscillatory synchronization / coherence-support behavior;

- recursive resonance continuity;

- resonance accessibility compression near criticality;

- and resonance-driven restructuring of operational trajectories.

## Operational Principle

Formation and retention of resonance-window synthesis cycles remain operationally constrained by:

`C(t) > P(t)`

over operational time.

Where:

- C(t) — general endogenous structural coherence parameter;

- P(t) — destabilizing structural pressure, fragmentation pressure, structural load, or dissipative maintenance cost.

Operational accessibility of resonance windows depends on:

- accumulated positive structural work;

- positive structural balance;

- coherent accumulation dynamics;

- endogenous drift conditions;

- recursive continuity preservation;

- retained operational accessibility;

- and the quality of endogenous structural coherence.

Synchronization or phase alignment may support resonance-window accessibility, but synchronization alone does not prove retained synthesis or real dynamic stability.

## Framework Interpretation

Within the framework, resonance windows represent:

- operational accessibility regions for structural self-organization;

- dynamically retained synthesis accessibility;

- operational phase-transition accessibility regions;

- and recursively inherited resonance continuity in nonlinear open dissipative dynamic systems.

A resonance window is not automatically positive.

Its qualitative sign depends on the direction and quality of endogenous drift.

A resonance window may therefore become:

- a synthesis window;

- a reorganization window;

- a degradation window;

- or a collapse window.

Only resonance windows that support accumulated positive structural work, retained-domain persistence, and preservation of `C(t) > P(t)` can contribute to dynamically retained synthesis.
