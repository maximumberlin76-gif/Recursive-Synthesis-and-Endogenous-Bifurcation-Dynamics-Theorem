# Core Canonical Distinctions

The repository depends on several mandatory distinctions.

## 1. Structural Balance vs Dynamic Stability

Structural balance:

`Δ(t) = S(t) − P(t) − D(t)`

Real dynamic stability:

`C(t) > P(t)`

These are not identical.

`Δ(t) > 0`

may support formal structural existence,

but it does not prove real dynamic stability.

## 2. Synchronization vs Coherence

Phase synchronization may support coherence.

But:

`R(t) ≠ C(t)`

R(t) is only a synchronization / coherence-support indicator.

C(t) is the general endogenous structural coherence parameter.

## 3. Resonance Window vs Synthesis

A resonance window gives access.

It does not prove synthesis.

Synthesis requires:

`Θ_N ≥ Θ_crit`

and retained persistence inside:

`Ω_ret`

## 4. Retention vs External Forcing

External forcing may support entry.

It does not prove retention.

Retention begins only when the system preserves organization after forcing is reduced or removed.

## 5. Quality vs Qualitative Characteristics

The repository uses:

`qualitative characteristics`

not:

`quality`

because the system inherits a concrete regime profile, not an abstract evaluation.

Qualitative characteristics may include:

- drift direction;

- attractor stability;

- basin accessibility;

- forward invariance;

- perturbation sensitivity;

- resonance-window accessibility;

- degree of retained coherence or fragmentation;

- accessibility of subsequent retained operational domains.
